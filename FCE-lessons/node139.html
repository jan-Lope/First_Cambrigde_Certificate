Modal verbs -- Obligation

The meaning of the various verbs can be summarised as follows:
Positive:
You must go   It's obligatory, you have no choice.
You have to go   It's obligatory, you have no choice.
You need to go   It's very advisable, but you choose.
you should go   It's very advisable, but you choose.
You ought to go   I advise you to go, but you choose.
You can go   If you want, you choose.
you may go   You have my permission to go.


Negative:
You mustn't go   It's forbidden, you have no choice.
You can go   It's forbidden, you have no choice.
you shouldn't so   I advise you not to go, but you choose.
you oughtn't to go   I advise you not to go, but you choose.
You don't have to go   It's not obligatory, you choose.
You don't need to go   It's not obligatory, you choose.
You needn't go   It's not necessary, you choose.
