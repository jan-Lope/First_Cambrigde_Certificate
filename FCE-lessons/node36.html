Causative have

This form exists in a variety of tenses and in each case it is the verb 'to have' which indicates the tense. The verb indicating the action is always the past participle.
I'm having my car repaired. (Someone is repairing my car.)
Compare with: I'm repairing my car. (This means I am repairing it myself.)

Causative have is formed by:
1) The subject = Sharon
2) The verb to have = is having
3) The object = her central heating
4) The past participle of the main verb = serviced.
Sharon is having her central heating serviced.
