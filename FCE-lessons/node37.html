Genitive 's

The genitive 's is mainly used to indicate possession of things by people, and family relationships. Add 's to singular nouns.
Sally's dog.
Sally and Peter's dog.
Nick's brother.

Add ' only to plural nouns endings in s.
The girls' books.
My grandparents' house.

Plural nouns not ending in s are an exception:
The children's books.

Animals, days and cities sometimes take the genitive. Most other objects do not:
The dog's basket.
Monday's lesson.
London's theatres.
but
The leg of the table.
The chair leg.
