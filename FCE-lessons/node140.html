Make, let, allow

Make + infinitive is used to express obligation.
They make you wear a uniform at my school. (active)
You are made to wear a uniform at my school. (passive)
Let + infinitive is used to express permission in the active:
They let you park here on Saturdays.
Allow + infinitive with to is used to express permission in the passive:
You are allowed to park here on Saturdays.
