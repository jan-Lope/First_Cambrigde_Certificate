Reporting verbs

Often the meaning of a sentence in direct speech is summarised by a verb in reported speech.
"Would you like to go to the cinema, Jane?" said Eric.
Eric invited Jane to the cinema.

There are three main types:

Type A: Verb + person + infinitive with "to".
He told me to sit down.

Type B: Verb + infinitive with "to".
She agreed to give him a reference.

Type C: Verb + -ing.
She regretted leaving her job.

He apologised for losing the report.
