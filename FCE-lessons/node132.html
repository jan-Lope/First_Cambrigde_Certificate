Exam Focus -- Use Of English

Complete the second sentence so that it has a similar meaning to the first sentence, using the word given. Do not change the word given.

"You've broken my radio, Frank!" said Jane. -- accused:
Jan ...................................... her radio.
Jan accused Frank of having broken her radio.

Susan regrets not buying that house. -- wishes:
Susan ...................................... that house.
Susan wishes she had bought that house. (conditional)

I could never have succeeded without your help. -- you:
I could never have succeeded ...................................... me.
I could never have succeeded if you hadn't helped me. (past perfect)

Linda's plans for a picnic have been spoilt by the weather. -- fallen:
Linda's plans for a picnic ...................................... because of the weather.
Linda's plans for a picnic have fallen through because of the weather.

Perhaps Brian went home early. -- may:
Brian ...................................... home early.
Brian may have gone home early.

The thief suddenly realised that the police were watching him. -- watched:
The thief suddenly realised that he ...................................... by the police.
The thief suddenly realised that he was been watched by the police.
