Although/Despite

These are used to indicate contrast.
Although is followed by a subject and verb:
Although there was very little snow, the skiing resort was crowded.
Despite is followed by a noun, a noun phrase or gerund + gerund + verb:
Despite the fact that it was cold, the central heating was turned off.
Despite the cold weather, we decided to climb the mountain.
Despite feeling cold, we decided to climb the mountain.
We can also say even though (although) -- in spite of (despite)
Although ... = Despite the fact ...
